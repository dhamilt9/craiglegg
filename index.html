Where To Stay

Lordes Camp

We've rented out the entire camp for the weekend, so everyone is welcome (and encouraged) to stay with us at the venue! Our goal is to make the weekend as fun and easy as possible for all of our friends and family. Staying on site means that you won't have to worry about post-party driving arrangements or pay for hotel! Furthermore you'll get to enjoy all of the lakeside activities that the camp has to offer.

A Few Things to Note:

  • Although staying on site is free, please be aware that the accommodations are relatively rustic. Relive your childhood summer camp experience with twin beds and a communal bathroom.

  • We ask that guests who will be driving and staying at the camp bring their own sheets and blankets. If you are flying, please let us know and we will try our best to make arrangements for you.

  • If you can't live without the amenities of a hotel, we would suggest booking a nearby room on Airbnb.com or VRBO.com. Otherwise the closest motels can be found on Rt. 20 in Skaneateles, the nearest hotels are in Cortland or Auburn (about 30/40 away minutes respectively). Please let us know as far in advance as possible if you would like to stay at the camp. There will be plenty of room, but we'd like a rough headcount for planning purposes.
What to Wear

We ask our guests to dress in a way that will work both indoors and outdoors in anticipation of unpredictable September weather. We suggest "Smart Casual" attire: a jacket or sport coat for men and dresses for women.

Later in the evening you may want to change into more comfortable clothing as you relax outside or sit beside the bonfire

Food and Booze

We will have an open bar serving beer and wine during the cocktail hour, dinner, and as long as supplies last afterwords. Snacks will be served during the cocktail hour immediately following the ceremony. When the couple is done with photos, we will move to the boathouse for a BBQ dinner.
For those staying at the camp (hopefully all of you!) please plan on bringing snacks or heading into town for lunch on the 24th before the festivities begin.
